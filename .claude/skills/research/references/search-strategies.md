# Search Strategies

## Boolean Operators

- **AND** — narrows results, requires all terms: `anxiety AND "college students" AND alcohol`
- **OR** — broadens with synonyms: `cardiomyopathy OR myocardiopathy OR "heart disease"`
- **NOT** — excludes terms (use cautiously, can drop relevant results)
- **Parentheses** — control order: `diet therapy AND (bulimia OR anorexia)`

**Query formulation process:** Break research question into 2–4 core concepts → identify synonyms for each → combine synonyms with OR → connect concepts with AND → start broad, narrow progressively.

## Google Advanced Operators

| Operator | Example | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| `"exact phrase"` | `"climate change adaptation"` | Exact phrase match |
| `site:` | `site:*.edu "your topic"` | Restrict to domain |
| `filetype:` | `filetype:pdf "market research"` | Find specific formats |
| `intitle:` | `intitle:"research methodology"` | Terms in page title |
| `AROUND(n)` | `Apple AROUND(3) phone` | Terms within n words |
| `before:` / `after:` | `after:2024-01-01` | Date range filtering |

Combine for precision: `site:*.gov "statistics" "your topic" filetype:csv`

## Google Scholar

- **Forward citation search**: "Cited by" link finds newer work building on a paper
- **Backward citation search**: Reference lists trace intellectual lineage
- **Library Links**: Configure in Settings for institutional access ("Find It @YourLibrary")
- **Alerts**: Click envelope icon after any search for email notifications on new matches

## Academic Databases

| Database | Coverage | Strength |
|---|---|---|
| **PubMed** | 37M citations, biomedical/life sciences | MeSH controlled vocabulary |
| **JSTOR** | 12M articles back to 1876 | Humanities, social sciences archives |
| **Web of Science** | 21,000 journals | Citation analysis, Journal Impact Factors |
| **Scopus** | 83M records | ~20% more citation coverage than WoS |
| **SSRN** | Social sciences, law, economics | Working papers and preprints |

No single database covers everything — use multiple for comprehensive research.

## Essential Research Tools

- **Wayback Machine** (web.archive.org) — 1T+ archived pages since 1996. Verify past page content, recover deleted content, track messaging changes. Courts accept as digital evidence. Limited on JavaScript-heavy/login-required sites (use web-browser skill for those).
- **Preprint servers** (arXiv, bioRxiv, medRxiv) — cutting-edge pre-peer-review research. Treat cautiously; always check if a peer-reviewed version exists before citing.
- **Citation chaining** (snowball method) — start with one relevant paper, check its references (backward), check "Cited by" (forward), repeat. Captures literature keyword searches miss.
- **Unpaywall** (browser extension) — finds free legal full-text of paywalled articles across 50K+ open access sources.
- **Zotero Connector** — one-click citation capture with automatic metadata; integrates with Retraction Watch Database.

## Government Data Sources

- **Data.gov** — U.S. federal open data
- **FRED** — 500K+ financial and economic data series
- **World Bank Open Data** — global development indicators
- **Google Dataset Search** — searches across thousands of data repositories
