# Octalysis Core Drives Reference

## The Eight Core Drives

### 1. Epic Meaning & Calling
**Definition:** The drive activated when a person believes they are doing something greater than themselves or were "chosen" to take that action.

**Brain Type:** Split evenly between Left Brain and Right Brain  
**Motivation Type:** White Hat (positive emotions)  
**Best Used In:** Discovery and Onboarding phases  
**Critical Factor:** Believability of the message is absolutely crucial

**Key Techniques:**
- Narrative (Technique #10)
- Humanity Hero (Technique #27)
- Elitism (Technique #26)
- Beginner's Luck (Technique #23)
- Free Lunch (Technique #24)

---

### 2. Development & Accomplishment
**Definition:** Internal drive for making progress, developing skills, achieving mastery, and overcoming challenges.

**Brain Type:** Left Brain  
**Motivation Type:** White Hat (positive emotions)  

**Key Techniques:**
- Progress Bars (Technique #4)
- Rockstar Effect (Technique #92)
- Achievement Symbols (Technique #2)
- Status Points (Technique #1)
- Leaderboards (Technique #3)

---

### 3. Empowerment of Creativity & Feedback
**Definition:** Engagement through creative process where users repeatedly figure out new things and try different combinations.

**Brain Type:** Right Brain  
**Motivation Type:** White Hat (positive emotions)  

**Key Techniques:**
- Boosters (Technique #31)
- Milestone Unlock (Technique #19)
- Poison Picker - Choice Perception (Technique #89)
- Plant Picker - Meaningful Choice (Technique #11)

---

### 4. Ownership & Possession
**Definition:** Motivation arising from feeling of owning or controlling something.

**Brain Type:** Strong Left Brain  
**Motivation Type:** Split between White Hat and Black Hat  

**Key Techniques:**
- Build from Scratch (Technique #43)
- Collection Sets (Technique #16)
- Exchangeable Points (Technique #75)
- Monitor Attachment (Technique #42)
- Alfred Effect (Technique #83)

---

### 5. Social Influence & Relatedness
**Definition:** All social elements that motivate people: mentorship, social acceptance, social feedback, companionship, competition, and envy.

**Brain Type:** Strong Right Brain  
**Motivation Type:** Split between White Hat and Black Hat  

**Key Techniques:**
- Mentorship (Technique #61)
- Brag Buttons (Technique #57)
- Trophy Shelves (Technique #64)
- Group Quests (Technique #22)
- Social Treasures (Technique #63)
- Social Prods (Technique #62)
- Conformity Anchor (Technique #58)
- Water Coolers (Technique #55)

---

### 6. Scarcity & Impatience
**Definition:** Wanting something simply because it is extremely rare, exclusive, or immediately unattainable.

**Brain Type:** Left Brain  
**Motivation Type:** Black Hat (negative emotions)  

**Note:** This drive creates urgency and desire through limitation.

---

### 7. Unpredictability & Curiosity
**Definition:** Constant engagement because you don't know what is going to happen next.

**Brain Type:** Right Brain  
**Motivation Type:** Black Hat (negative emotions)  

**Note:** Keeps users engaged through mystery and surprise.

---

### 8. Loss & Avoidance
**Definition:** Motivation to avoid something negative from happening.

**Brain Type:** Split between Left Brain and Right Brain  
**Motivation Type:** Strongly Black Hat (negative emotions)  

**Note:** Most powerful but potentially harmful if overused.

---

### 9. Sensation (Hidden Ninth Drive)
**Definition:** Physical feelings and sensations.

**Note:** Often overlooked but important for physical/haptic experiences.

---

## Brain Categorization

### Left Brain Core Drives (Extrinsic Motivation)
Tend toward wanting to attain something you don't (or can't) have:
1. Epic Meaning & Calling (split)
2. Development & Accomplishment
4. Ownership & Possession
6. Scarcity & Impatience
8. Loss & Avoidance (split)

### Right Brain Core Drives (Intrinsic Motivation)
Tend toward internal satisfaction and creativity:
1. Epic Meaning & Calling (split)
3. Empowerment of Creativity & Feedback
5. Social Influence & Relatedness
7. Unpredictability & Curiosity
8. Loss & Avoidance (split)

---

## Motivation Categorization

### White Hat Core Drives (Positive Emotions)
Provide sense of agency and control:
1. Epic Meaning & Calling
2. Development & Accomplishment
3. Empowerment of Creativity & Feedback
4. Ownership & Possession (split)
5. Social Influence & Relatedness (split)

### Black Hat Core Drives (Negative Emotions)
Create sense of helplessness and lack of control:
4. Ownership & Possession (split)
5. Social Influence & Relatedness (split)
6. Scarcity & Impatience
7. Unpredictability & Curiosity
8. Loss & Avoidance
