# Research Brief

## Research Question

What lessons can we learn from the FATE RPG system for designing a QBN (quality-based narrative) game engine? Specifically: how do FATE's free-form tagging (aspects), compels, invocations, and fate points map to QBN mechanics — and what design patterns should we adopt, adapt, or avoid?

## Prior Knowledge / Constraints

- Building a text-only MMORPG using QBN (Failbetter/Fallen London style storylet engine)
- Already researched: Perko's yarnball concept (interlocking meters as narrative generators), Sunless Sea/Skies economy, Traveller economy, MMORPG economy design
- QBN uses qualities (numeric values) to gate storylet availability; storylets modify qualities
- The yarnball model uses interconnected soft challenges (meters) that produce emergent narrative
- Key question: FATE uses free-form text tags (aspects) rather than numeric qualities — what's gained and lost?

## Priority Aspects

- Practical design patterns transferable to a digital QBN system
- Expert analysis of FATE's strengths and weaknesses
- Comparison of free-form tagging vs numeric qualities
- How compels create narrative pressure (relates to yarnball)

## Output Format

Structured briefing

## Complexity Tier

Comparison

## Done Criteria

1. Core FATE mechanics documented (aspects, fate points, compels, invocations, stunts)
2. Free-form tagging (aspects) explained with design rationale and examples
3. At least 2 concrete mappings between FATE mechanics and QBN qualities/storylets
4. Analysis of what FATE does well for emergent narrative that QBN could adopt
5. Analysis of what doesn't translate to digital/MMO or has limitations

## Search Angles

1. **FATE core mechanics and design philosophy** — aspects, fate points, compels, invocations, stunts, the FATE fractal, and why Evil Hat designed it this way
2. **FATE lessons for digital narrative games** — analysis of FATE's narrative mechanics from a game design perspective, what works in tabletop vs digital, prior attempts to adapt FATE-like mechanics to video games
