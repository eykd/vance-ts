# Interstellar Sport & Competition — Oikumene & Beyond

---

## A. Types of Competition
- **Athletic Games:** Grav-ball, zero-g wrestling, endurance races in partial gravity arenas.
- **Martial Tournaments:** Sword, pike, and beam dueling under strict codes; some lethal, others ceremonial.
- **Starship Races:** Multi-system courier challenges or smuggler’s runs through hazardous corridors.
- **Mind & Strategy Games:** Holo-chess, narrative dueling, tactical simulations.
- **Religious Contests:** Pilgrim trials blending endurance, moral tests, and acts of charity.

---

## B. Cultural Functions
- **Diplomatic Arenas:** Neutral ground to resolve disputes or forge alliances.
- **Soft Power Projection:** Worlds display cultural excellence and tech superiority through elite teams.
- **Cultural Exchange:** Athletes spread art, fashion, and ideas alongside sport.
- **Ritual & Tradition:** Annual contests tied to feast days, anniversaries, or historic treaties.

---

## C. Organization & Governance
- **Oikumene Games Council:** Oversees rules, scheduling, and disputes for sanctioned events.
- **Independent Circuits:** Beyond leagues with looser safety rules.
- **Syndicate-Sponsored Events:** Fixed matches and blood sports for black-market gambling.
- **Church-Approved Competitions:** Emphasize virtue, discipline, and avoidance of scandal.

---

## D. Beyond Practices
- **High-Risk Wagers:** Athletes bet freedom, ships, or planetary rights.
- **Open-Class Races:** No rules on modifications or outside help; sabotage expected.
- **Spectacle over Safety:** Blood sports designed for crowd frenzy.

---

## E. Narrative Leverage
- Champion defects to rival bloc, sparking diplomatic incident.
- Starship race doubles as cover for covert courier mission.
- Church-approved competition sabotaged by cultists to disgrace patron world.
