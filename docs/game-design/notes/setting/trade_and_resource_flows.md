# Trade & Resource Flows — Oikumene & Beyond

---

## A. Trade Architecture
- **Corridor Hubs:** High-traffic systems serving as customs, finance, and redistribution points.
- **Feeder Routes:** Secondary lines linking smaller systems to major hubs.
- **Beyond Trade Chains:** Barter, smuggling, and semi-legal exchanges connecting frontier outposts to Oikumene markets.
- **Seasonal Routes:** Corridors open or safe only during certain cosmic conditions.

---

## B. Resource Categories
- **Strategic Materials:** Rare isotopes, exotic alloys, corridor-stabilizing elements.
- **Luxury Goods:** Spices, art, rare wines, grav-silk garments.
- **Staple Commodities:** Grain, protein blocks, industrial feedstock.
- **Technological Components:** Navigation cores, shield projectors, medical nanites.
- **Cultural & Religious Items:** Pilgrimage relics, sacred art, scripture copies.

---

## C. Trade Governance
- **Guild Oversight:** Monopoly control over key goods; fixed pricing and quotas.
- **Bloc Tariffs:** Import/export duties used to protect industries or pressure rivals.
- **Church Tithe Stations:** Religious levy points collecting a fraction of goods for ecclesiastical works.
- **Treaty Ports:** Neutral ground for commerce between hostile powers.

---

## D. Economic Dynamics
- **Price Cascades:** Corridor disruptions ripple through markets, collapsing prices in one region and spiking them in another.
- **Speculative Runs:** Merchants gamble on demand surges due to political or environmental crises.
- **Credit Webs:** Debt and finance networks spanning systems, backed by guild banks or Church treasuries.
- **Trade as Diplomacy:** Resource flows used to cement alliances or punish dissent.

---

## E. Beyond Practices
- **Shadow Markets:** Parallel trade systems run by syndicates, smugglers, free captains.
- **Spoiler Tactics:** Beyond factions flood markets with inferior or counterfeit goods.
- **Gift Economies:** Frontier cultures valuing prestige and obligation over direct payment.

---

## F. Narrative Leverage
- Seasonal corridor closure traps a fleet carrying strategic materials.
- Guild manipulates famine for political leverage against a bloc.
- Church tithe station attacked, scattering relics into the black market.
