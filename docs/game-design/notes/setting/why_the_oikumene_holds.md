# Why the Oikumene Holds — Checks on Conquest

---

## 1. Geography & Physics Work Against Conquest
- **Travel Latency:** Even with broad, shifting safe corridors, moving a large fleet is slow, obvious, and tied to seasonal windows. By the time a conquering fleet arrives, other worlds have had weeks to prepare or rally allies.
- **Multiple Axes of Approach:** Many worlds have more than one viable route in or out, making sieges costly and porous.
- **Fragile Supply Lines:** Large fleets require fuel, spool maintenance, and consumables; in the frontier, these can’t be reliably sourced without local cooperation.

---

## 2. Mutual Deterrence
- **Distributed Armed Forces:** Most systems maintain armed patrols or defensive fleets capable of inflicting real losses.
- **Retaliatory Coalitions:** Mutual aid treaties mean aggression risks fighting multiple neighbors at once.
- **Privateer Letters:** In wartime, blocs deputize private captains to raid the aggressor’s shipping, creating a constant drain.

---

## 3. Economic Interdependence
- **Trade Web Dependence:** Worlds specialize in key exports; conquest risks breaking supply chains essential to the aggressor.
- **Credit Clearing Leverage:** Financial hubs like Aurivault can freeze or void the accounts of aggressors, cutting off payment to crews and suppliers.
- **Guild Sanctions:** Interstellar trading guilds can embargo aggressors, depriving them of essential imports.

---

## 4. Cultural & Political Norms
- **Legal Framework:** Oikumene law prohibits unprovoked interstellar aggression; violators lose treaty protections and mutual defense pacts.
- **Honor & Reputation:** Aggressors suffer lasting prestige damage, making future diplomacy difficult.
- **Profit Over Territory:** Leaders often find manipulating trade and treaties more lucrative than direct rule.

---

## 5. Strategic Wildcards
- **Beyond’s Proximity:** Overreach leaves borders vulnerable to pirates and warlords.
- **Scout Corps Factor:** Scouts report massing fleets quickly; surprise is rare.
- **Church Influence:** While not a fleet power, the Church can rally systems morally and culturally against aggressors.

---

## Setting Implication
The Oikumene’s balance is deliberately unstable: each world remains independent enough to preserve its identity, yet interlinked enough to make conquest unattractive. Real danger lies in slow, subtle accumulation of influence through debt, guild patronage, and treaties—until annexation is a fait accompli without a single shot fired.
