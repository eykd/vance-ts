# Religion & Ideological Movements Outside the Church — Oikumene & Beyond

---

## A. Syncretic & Fringe Faiths
- **Hero-Saint Cults:** Veneration of historical figures elevated to semi-divine status.
- **Mystic Guild Orders:** Trade associations wrapped in ritual, claiming divine sanction for commerce.
- **Corridor Animism:** Belief that each corridor has a spirit requiring offerings for safe passage.
- **Hybrid Pilgrimage Paths:** Journeys combining Church shrines with local sacred sites, often diluting doctrine.

---

## B. Secular & Philosophical Movements
- **Rationalist Concords:** Reject supernatural belief; venerate reason, science, and order as ultimate goods.
- **Ethical Hedonism Leagues:** Preach self-fulfillment through pleasure, restrained only by mutual consent.
- **Utilitarian Pacifists:** Advocate universal disarmament and trade as sole engines of civilization.
- **Machine Ascensionists:** Seek to merge human consciousness with synthetic systems for immortality.

---

## C. Beyond Spiritualities
- **Cargo Resurgence Faiths:** Frontier cults treating Oikumene technology as divine gifts.
- **Blood Oath Brotherhoods:** Warrior religions venerating combat as sacred expression.
- **Cosmic Mystery Schools:** Esoteric traditions claiming access to pre-human wisdom hidden in the stars.

---

## D. Relationship with the Church
- **Syncretic Tension:** Some sects claim compatibility with Church teaching while quietly subverting it.
- **Open Heresy:** Doctrinally incompatible movements condemned by ecclesiastical councils.
- **Pragmatic Coexistence:** On dangerous worlds, missionaries tolerate heterodox practice to maintain influence.
- **Conversion Rivalries:** Active competition for adherents in the same system.

---

## E. Narrative Leverage
- Corridor animist sect sabotages a navigation beacon to “appease the spirit.”
- Guild-led mystic order challenges the Church’s tithe rights in its colonies.
- Missionary accused of secretly leading a syncretic cult to convert hostile populations.
