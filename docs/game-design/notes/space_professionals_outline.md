# Space Professionals Pulp Story Outline - Lester Dent Formula
*Professional Western Structure in Space Opera Setting*
*Following the 12-Element Professional Western Structure*

## Story Foundation (Space Professionals Genre)

**Murder Method:** Sophisticated elimination technique using space technology (orbital bombardment disguised as meteor strikes, coordinated ambushes using cloaked ships, systematic assassination via hacked life support, weaponized nanobots in supply shipments)  
**Villain's Goal:** Control of valuable galactic territory/resource through organized fleet-scale operation  
**Setting:** Frontier space region where normal galactic law enforcement is overwhelmed by scale of criminal space armada  
**Hero's Truth:** "Professionals honor their contracts across all star systems" / "The team comes first in the void" / "Competence and loyalty matter more than galactic law"  
**Menace:** Villain's space fleet will establish permanent criminal empire unless stopped by equally professional opposition  

### Core Themes
- **Professionalism vs. Amateurism:** Skilled space experts against overwhelming but disorganized force
- **Crew Loyalty vs. Individual Gain:** Team bonds stronger than personal interest across the galaxy  
- **Competence vs. Authority:** Skills matter more than official galactic position or social status

---

## Chapter 1: Assembling the Crew (1,500 words)
*Elements 1-4: Professional Status, Contract, Opposition, and Context*

### Scene 1: The Job Offer
- **Goal:** Client presents seemingly straightforward professional space contract to lead hero
- **Conflict:** Job appears more dangerous and complex than initially described
- **Disaster:** Reveals that heroes are being hired because normal galactic authorities have completely failed **(Elements 1-2: Heroes are professionals hired for money)**

### Sequel 1: Assessing the Challenge
- **Reaction:** Lead hero recognizes this requires exceptional crew of space specialists
- **Dilemma:** Take dangerous but lucrative job vs. pass on potential disaster
- **Decision:** Accepts contract but only if he can assemble his own crew

### Scene 2: Recruiting the Specialists
- **Goal:** Gather crew of professionals, each with unique essential space skills
- **Conflict:** Each potential crew member has reasons to refuse the dangerous job
- **Disaster:** **First Twist** - discovers the true scope of villain's space fleet and galactic society's helplessness **(Elements 3-4: Villains very strong, society ineffective)**

---

## Chapter 2: The Bonds of Space War (1,500 words)
*Elements 5-8: Combat Engagement and Crew Dynamics*

### Sequel 2: Understanding the Mission
- **Reaction:** Crew realizes they're not just hired guns but last hope against organized space evil
- **Dilemma:** Complete job as contracted vs. expand mission to truly solve problem
- **Decision:** Commits to full engagement knowing it means space war **(Element 5: Job involves heroes in fight)**

### Scene 3: First Pinch Point - Testing Under Fire
- **Goal:** Initial space engagement to test villain's defenses and crew coordination
- **Conflict:** Each crew member must prove their special abilities in space combat
- **Disaster:** Crew's individual skills are impressive but villain's space fleet is even stronger than expected **(Element 6: Heroes have special abilities and status)**

### Sequel 3: Forging the Crew
- **Reaction:** Respect grows between crew members as each proves competence under space pressure
- **Dilemma:** Work as individual specialists vs. truly function as unified space crew
- **Decision:** Chooses to operate as genuine crew rather than collection of individuals **(Element 7: Heroes form group for job)**

### Scene 4: The Crew Bonds
- **Goal:** Execute complex space operation requiring perfect coordination between specialists
- **Conflict:** Mission success depends on absolute trust and mutual support in hostile void
- **Disaster:** **Midpoint Twist** - crew members risk their lives for each other in space, proving deep loyalty **(Element 8: Heroes share respect, affection, loyalty)**

---

## Chapter 3: Beyond the Contract (1,500 words)
*Elements 9-10: Independence and Major Engagement*

### Sequel 4: More Than Money
- **Reaction:** Crew realizes they care more about each other than the original space contract
- **Dilemma:** Complete job as hired vs. do what's right regardless of payment
- **Decision:** Chooses to operate independently of client's wishes and galactic society's expectations **(Element 9: Heroes independent of society)**

### Scene 5: The Professional Space War
- **Goal:** Launch major coordinated space assault on villain's fleet
- **Conflict:** Crew's skills and coordination against villain's superior numbers and resources
- **Disaster:** Initial success but villain's response threatens to overwhelm crew

### Scene 6: Second Pinch Point - Everything on the Line
- **Goal:** Push forward despite increasing odds and mounting space casualties
- **Conflict:** Crew must use every skill and risk everything to achieve victory **(Element 10: Heroes fight villains)**
- **Disaster:** **Big Setback** - Major crew member killed or ship captured; mission appears doomed

---

## Chapter 4: Brothers in the Void (1,500 words)
*Elements 11-12: Victory and Ultimate Loyalty*

### Sequel 5: The Final Bond
- **Reaction:** Loss of crew member proves that group loyalty transcends individual survival in space
- **Dilemma:** Cut losses and escape vs. complete mission regardless of cost
- **Decision:** Crew commits to victory or death together among the stars

### Scene 7: The Professional Solution
- **Goal:** Use specialized skills and perfect teamwork to defeat superior enemy space fleet
- **Conflict:** Each crew member sacrifices for the others in coordinated final space assault
- **Disaster:** Crew achieves victory but at tremendous personal cost **(Element 11: Heroes defeat villains)**

### Scene 8: Honor Among Space Professionals
- **Goal:** Ensure crew's sacrifice achieves lasting victory over villain space organization
- **Conflict:** Surviving crew members must decide their future
- **Disaster:** Mission complete but crew chooses loyalty to each other over any other consideration

### Final Sequel: The Professional's Code
- **Reaction:** Job is finished, space villain defeated, but crew bonds are permanent
- **Dilemma:** Return to individual careers vs. stay together as permanent space unit
- **Decision:** Crew remains together, bound by shared experience and mutual loyalty **(Element 12: Heroes stay together)**
- **Punch Line:** Professionals fly toward next star system, not as hired guns but as brothers who happen to be very good at space warfare

## Space Professionals Genre Specifications

### The 12 Essential Elements Integration (Space Context)

**Elements 1-4 (Chapter 1):** Professional status, monetary contract, overwhelming space opposition, galactic societal failure
**Elements 5-8 (Chapter 2):** Space combat engagement, individual abilities, crew formation, team bonding  
**Elements 9-12 (Chapter 3-4):** Independence from galactic society, major space conflict, victory, permanent loyalty

### Crew Composition (Essential Space Specialists)

#### The Captain
- **Skills:** Space strategy, crew coordination, inspiration, decision-making under combat pressure
- **Background:** Former military fleet officer, experienced mercenary commander, natural leader
- **Role:** Holds crew together and makes final tactical decisions in space battles

#### The Ace Pilot
- **Skills:** Unmatched ship handling, space combat reflexes, fighter craft mastery
- **Background:** Former military ace, professional racer, reformed space pirate
- **Role:** Handles direct space confrontations and provides escort/attack capabilities

#### The Engineer/Demolitions Expert
- **Skills:** Ship systems, explosives, engineering, mechanical knowledge, technical sabotage
- **Background:** Former naval engineer, mining specialist, anarchist
- **Role:** Handles ship maintenance, creates diversions, breaches space defenses

#### The Navigator/Scout
- **Skills:** Hyperspace navigation, reconnaissance, stealth operations, reading space terrain
- **Background:** Former exploration service, smuggler, deep space guide
- **Role:** Gathers intelligence, finds routes through hostile space, warns of danger

#### The Communications/Infiltration Specialist
- **Skills:** Electronic warfare, hacking, social manipulation, information gathering
- **Background:** Former intelligence officer, con artist, corporate spy
- **Role:** Gets crew inside enemy territory, provides false identities, electronic support

### Villain Organization (Space Context)
- **Structure:** Fleet hierarchy with clear chain of command across multiple ships
- **Resources:** Superior numbers, better ships, territorial control of star systems
- **Advantage:** Organization and coordination vs. individual skills
- **Weakness:** Rigid structure vulnerable to unconventional space tactics

### Galactic Society's Role
- **Position:** Completely outmatched by villain's space fleet organization
- **Response:** Hires professionals because galactic military/police solutions have failed
- **Relationship:** Dependent on heroes but unable to understand or control them
- **Outcome:** Saved by heroes but remains fundamentally weak

### Professional Code Elements (Space Context)

#### Competence Above All
- Crew members respect space skills regardless of background or morality
- Professional ability matters more than galactic social standing or legal status
- Each member must prove worth through performance under space pressure

#### Contract Honor
- Job accepted must be completed regardless of personal cost
- Original agreement sacred even when circumstances change across star systems
- Professional reputation depends on keeping word throughout the galaxy

#### Crew Loyalty
- Group bonds transcend individual interests or safety in the void
- Members will risk/sacrifice lives for each other in space combat
- Crew becomes family united by shared danger and mutual dependence

#### Independence from Galactic Society
- Professionals operate by their own moral code among the stars
- Galactic society's rules don't apply to those who risk everything in space
- Group answers only to itself and professional standards

### Key Space Professional Themes

#### Competence vs. Authority
- **Chapter 1:** Official galactic authority has failed where professionals might succeed
- **Chapter 2:** Individual space skills prove superior to organizational hierarchy
- **Chapter 3:** Crew coordination defeats superior numbers through space expertise
- **Chapter 4:** Professional bonds stronger than galactic legal or social obligations

#### Individual vs. Group
- **Chapter 1:** Specialists recruited for individual space abilities
- **Chapter 2:** Individual skills must be coordinated for group success in space
- **Chapter 3:** Group identity becomes more important than individual survival
- **Chapter 4:** Crew loyalty transcends all other considerations

#### Professional vs. Personal
- **Chapter 1:** Job taken for money and professional space challenge
- **Chapter 2:** Personal relationships develop through shared space danger
- **Chapter 3:** Professional standards demand completion regardless of cost
- **Chapter 4:** Personal loyalty makes professional bond permanent

### Murder Methods (Space Professional)
- **Coordinated Fleet Elimination:** Systematic removal of resistance using multiple ships
- **Technical Space Sabotage:** Sabotaged life support, navigation, or propulsion systems
- **Military Space Tactics:** Ambushes, sieges, strategic elimination using superior firepower
- **Psychological Space Warfare:** Terror campaigns using orbital bombardment threats
- **Inside Jobs:** Corruption and betrayal by trusted crew or officials

### Villain Goals (Space Professional)
- **Territorial Control:** Establishing criminal empire over star systems
- **Resource Monopoly:** Controlling asteroid mining, hyperspace routes, or rare materials
- **Political Power:** Installing puppet governments through space-based intimidation
- **Criminal Network:** Building organized crime syndicate across multiple systems
- **Military Conquest:** Using superior fleet to establish new galactic order

### Special Abilities (Space Crew Members)
- **Leadership:** Inspiring loyalty and coordinating complex space operations
- **Combat Mastery:** Superior fighting skills in multiple space environments
- **Technical Expertise:** Specialized knowledge others lack about space systems
- **Survival Skills:** Thriving in hostile space environment and alien worlds
- **Social Manipulation:** Reading and controlling people across different cultures

### Mission Structure (Space Context)

#### Phase 1: Intelligence Gathering
- Reconnaissance of enemy fleet strength and positions
- Identification of key targets and space vulnerabilities
- Assessment of local space terrain and resources

#### Phase 2: Preparation
- Acquiring necessary ships, equipment and space supplies
- Establishing safe harbors and escape routes through hyperspace
- Coordinating crew roles and backup plans

#### Phase 3: Initial Engagement
- Testing enemy responses and space capabilities
- Eliminating outer defenses and patrol ships
- Gathering final intelligence for main space assault

#### Phase 4: Final Assault
- Coordinated attack using all crew abilities
- Systematic destruction of enemy space organization
- Escape and consolidation of victory

### Scene Types by Chapter (Space Context)

#### Chapter 1 Scenes
- **Contract Negotiation:** Client explains space job and payment
- **Crew Recruitment:** Gathering specialists with needed space skills
- **Mission Briefing:** Understanding true scope of space challenge

#### Chapter 2 Scenes
- **Skills Testing:** Each member proves abilities under space pressure
- **Crew Building:** Learning to work together effectively in space
- **First Contact:** Initial engagement with enemy space forces

#### Chapter 3 Scenes
- **Major Space Operation:** Coordinated assault on enemy fleet
- **Crew Crisis:** Loss of member tests group bonds in space
- **Point of No Return:** Commitment to victory or death among stars

#### Chapter 4 Scenes
- **Final Space Battle:** All skills and loyalty tested in climactic fleet engagement
- **Victory Celebration:** Mission accomplished but at great cost
- **New Beginning:** Crew chooses permanent partnership

### Supporting Characters (Space Context)

#### The Client
- **Role:** Provides mission and payment but limited understanding of space warfare
- **Function:** Represents galactic society's dependence on professionals
- **Arc:** Learns to respect crew's methods and sacrifice

#### The Inside Contact
- **Role:** Local source of information and assistance in target system
- **Function:** Bridge between crew and local space situation
- **Arc:** May betray or prove loyal under pressure

#### The Enemy Fleet Commander
- **Role:** Skilled opponent who matches crew's space abilities
- **Function:** Personal antagonist who tests crew coordination
- **Arc:** Respects professionals even while fighting them

### Space Combat Elements
- **Ship Mastery:** Starship operations emphasizing skill and teamwork
- **Fleet Fighting:** Battles against enemy formations using superior coordination
- **Environmental Combat:** Using asteroid fields, nebulae, and space phenomena as weapons
- **Boarding Actions:** Hand-to-hand combat aboard enemy vessels
- **Tactical Thinking:** Combining individual instincts with strategic space planning

### Space Technology Elements
- **Fleet Coordination:** Managing multiple ships in complex space operations
- **Electronic Warfare:** Hacking enemy systems and communications
- **Hyperspace Tactics:** Using faster-than-light travel strategically
- **Orbital Mechanics:** Understanding space physics for tactical advantage
- **Alien Technology:** Incorporating non-human tech for mission success

### Pacing Notes (Space Professional)

- **Methodical Buildup:** Careful crew assembly and preparation phase
- **Escalating Complexity:** Each space engagement more challenging than last
- **Relationship Development:** Crew bonds strengthen through shared space danger
- **Explosive Climax:** All skills and loyalty tested simultaneously in space

The 6,000-word structure allows for both the methodical professional approach and the emotional crew bonding that makes the final sacrifice meaningful, now projected across the vastness of space where the crew's transformation from hired guns to brothers-in-arms takes on epic galactic scope.
